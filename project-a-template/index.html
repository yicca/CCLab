[ EMOTERIA ]

Scientific Name:

EMOTERIA

Discovered by / at:

Rebecca, Feb 24, 2025

Family:

Unknown

Habitat:

Human Brain

Appearance:

The emoteria 'breathes', with its size changing slowly. It remains monochrome / grey if it's unnoticed, but if the host feeds it with attention, it will change its colors based on the host's mood.

Key Characteristics:

  • Wanders through the mind
  • Usually feeds on attention
  • Feeds on the brain after mutation

What we know so far about Emoteria? The Monochrome Age began 3 years ago, when more than 80% of humans suddenly lost the ability to perceive color due to Monochrome Syndrome. Most patients were subjected to exploitation, which is suspected to be the cause of the disease. Scientists discovered Emoteria in those who retained their color vision and extracted it from their brains for further research and medical study. Recently, they confirmed that Emoteria reside at the core of the human brain and randomly appears in other parts of it. Several characteristics are identified. However, Emoteria can only be seen when the host consciously awakens them, which makes deeper research more challenging.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Healthy Emoteria should remain fully under the host's control. The host can choose to ignore it or focus on it at will. However, long time of neglect may lead to mutation. To maintain its stability, please provide it with a proper amount of attention each day. The flower serves as a signal of its health.
